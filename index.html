Bio

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Applied Mathematics and Statistics at Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, Maryland, USA. My research focuses on optimization and machine learning, particularly in developing new methods to solve large-scale problems efficiently, under the supervision of Mateo Díaz.

I completed my MSc in Computer Science at Université de Montréal, Québec, Canada, where I worked with Andrea Lodi and Didier Chetelat. In addition, I obtained a double major BSc in Computer Science and Mathematics from Université de Montréal. During my undergraduate studies, I gained industry experience as a Software Engineer Intern at Noviflow Inc. in Montreal, working on web app development and data visualization projects. I also contributed to machine learning engineering tasks as an intern at the CERIST Agency in Algeria.

Research

My research focuses on optimization and statistics for machine learning. Some of my current projects include:

  • Inverse Optimization Techniques for Large Language Model Interpretability: Investigating new techniques to improve the interpretability of large models.
  • Novel Optimization Algorithm for Composite Machine Learning: Developing new algorithms to improve the scalability of machine learning problems.

Publications
